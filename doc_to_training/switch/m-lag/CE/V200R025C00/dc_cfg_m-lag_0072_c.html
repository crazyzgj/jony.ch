Configuring V-VBST

Context

Virtual VLAN-based Spanning Tree (V-VBST) virtualizes two VBST-enabled devices into one device to perform VBST calculation. (For details about VBST, see Understanding VBST.) VBST can detect the M-LAG master/backup negotiation status. After V-VBST is enabled on the M-LAG master and backup devices and M-LAG master/backup negotiation is successful, the two devices are virtualized into one device for port role calculation and fast convergence calculation. VBST needs to synchronize the bridge MAC addresses and instance priorities of the M-LAG master and backup devices. After M-LAG master/backup negotiation is successful, the M-LAG backup device uses the bridge MAC address and instance priority that are synchronized from the M-LAG master device for VBST calculation and packet transmission. This ensures consistent VBST parameter settings on the two M-LAG devices that are virtualized into one logical device.

Currently, V-VBST can be used only in M-LAG networking to meet the requirements of multi-level M-LAG interconnection and M-LAG devices functioning as non-root bridges.

When configuring V-VBST, ensure that the VBST timer settings of the same VLAN on the two devices in an M-LAG are the same. Otherwise, network flapping may occur.
